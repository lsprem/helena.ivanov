The 4-Phase Propaganda Model

Phase I: Narrative Shaping

Identifying "Others" and framing them as a threat to the core identity. Strategic use of binary discourse.

Phase II: Historical Weaponization

Manipulating historical grievances to justify a moral imperative for revenge or pre-emptive action.

Phase III: Dehumanization

The transition of language to strip targets of human agency, lowering moral barriers to violence.

Phase IV: Mobilization

The final active phase where the state or actor leverages normalized hate into physical, organized conflict.

"An essential framework for understanding how the written and spoken word becomes the engine of real-world violence."
